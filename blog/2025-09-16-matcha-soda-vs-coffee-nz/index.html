Matcha Soda: A Coffee Alternative

Matcha is a finely ground green tea powder made from shade-grown tea leaves. Unlike regular green tea, you consume the entire leaf — meaning more antioxidants, more nutrients, and a smoother caffeine experience.

In New Zealand, interest in matcha keeps growing. Searches like matcha latte NZ, matcha powder NZ, and matcha Auckland show that people are looking for alternatives to the daily flat white. Turning matcha into a soda was the breakthrough for me.

What Does Matcha Taste Like?

People often ask: “What does matcha taste like?”

  • Earthy and grassy
  • Slightly sweet and creamy
  • Umami, not bitter (if it’s high quality)

In soda form, matcha becomes lighter and more refreshing — slightly sweet, a little tangy, and very easy to drink.

Matcha vs Coffee: Caffeine Comparison

This is one of the most searched questions in NZ:

  • Double-shot coffee: ~120–150 mg caffeine
  • Matcha soda (500 ml): ~60–90 mg caffeine

Coffee delivers more caffeine upfront. Matcha delivers it differently.

Why Matcha Feels Smoother Than Coffee

Matcha naturally contains L-theanine, an amino acid that changes how caffeine affects your body.

  • Promotes calm, focused energy
  • Reduces jitters and anxiety
  • Slows caffeine absorption
  • Helps avoid energy crashes

Instead of a spike-and-crash, matcha provides steady energy for 4–6 hours.

Matcha Soda vs Matcha Latte

Most Kiwis are introduced to matcha through a matcha latte. Lattes are comforting — but they’re often heavy and milk-based.

Matcha soda is different:

  • Light, fizzy, and refreshing
  • Dairy-free and vegan
  • Lower in calories
  • Perfect for summer or afternoons

If a matcha latte is comfort food, matcha soda is the everyday energy drink alternative.
